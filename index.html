A Dynasty Begins

K'uhul Ajaw

Lord of the Jungle Cities

You rule a small Maya city-state deep in the lowland jungle. Your fields are narrow, your warriors few, and your name unknown beyond the nearest river bend.

To the east, the city of Ox Witz has grown fat on trade and slave labour. Their walls climb above the canopy. They have never paid tribute to anyone.

You will change that — if your farmers can feed your army, and your army can survive the march.

How to Play

  1. 1 Farm. Walk the fields, talk to your farmers. Assign them as warriors or porters at the temple. Crops grow through the dry season — more farmers, more food.
  2. 2 Plan the march. The temple shows how far it is, how much food you'll need, and whether your porters can carry enough. Don't march short.
  3. 3 March. Events on the road drain food and morale. Every choice matters — especially when a porter deserts.
  4. 4 Fight. Your surviving warriors storm Ox Witz. Win and tribute carts will roll home. Lose and you walk back empty.
  5. 5 Watch the tribute. It won't last long. When the insult comes, you decide whether to march again — into doubled walls.
